About Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is the use of digital platforms to harass, threaten, or humiliate others. It can have serious emotional and psychological effects on victims.

Real-world Example: The Amanda Todd Case

Amanda Todd, a Canadian teenager, became a symbol of the dangers of cyberbullying after she posted a YouTube video describing her experience. Her story raised global awareness about the impact of online harassment and the importance of support and intervention.

Bystander Effect

The bystander effect is a social phenomenon where individuals are less likely to help a victim when others are present. In online spaces, this can mean ignoring or failing to report harmful behavior, allowing bullying to escalate.
