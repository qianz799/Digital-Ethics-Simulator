Price of Free

Many "free" apps and services follow a business model where the user is not the customer, but the product. Instead of charging money, these companies collect vast amounts of data about your activities, preferences, and personal information.

Real-world Example: The Weather App Scandal

In recent years, numerous popular weather applications on both iOS and Android have been found to be collecting and selling their users' precise location data. An investigation into The Weather Channel app, for instance, revealed it was sending its users' minute-by-minute location data to third parties for commercial purposes, including hedge fund analysis and targeted advertising. This happened in the background, often without the users' full awareness of how their sensitive data was being monetized.

How to Protect Yourself

Taking control of your data often starts with your phone's settings. Regularly reviewing which apps have access to your information is a crucial step in protecting your digital privacy. You have the power to grant or deny permissions on an app-by-app basis.
